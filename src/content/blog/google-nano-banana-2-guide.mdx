## What Makes It Different

Nano Banana 2 isn't just another image generator. It's built on Gemini 3.1 Flash's reasoning backbone — meaning it **thinks before it renders**.

### The 6 Core Capabilities

**1. Planning Before Pixels**
It reasons about physics, object interactions, spatial relationships, and composition before generating anything. This eliminates most of the "weird hands" and "floating objects" problems.

**2. Real-Time Web Grounding**
It can pull live data from Google Search to create accurate infographics, weather charts, and depictions of real-world subjects. No other image model does this.

**3. Text That Actually Works**
It renders legible, stylized text in any language. Marketing mockups, greeting cards, infographics — text that doesn't look like alphabet soup.

**4. Character Consistency**
Maintain the same character across multiple images. Up to 5 characters with resemblance, up to 14 reference images total. Storyboarding, product catalogs, brand assets — finally possible.

**5. Native 4K + 14 Aspect Ratios**
From 512px to 4K. Aspect ratios include 1:1, 16:9, 9:16, 21:9, and everything in between. No upscaling tricks.

**6. Flash Speed at Pro Quality**
Under 2 seconds for standard resolution. API cost: ~$0.067 per 2K image (half of competitors).

## The Structured Prompting Framework

This is the difference between amateur and professional results.

**The Formula:**
```
Subject — What is the main focus
Composition — Camera angle, framing, distance
Action — What is happening
Location — Where it takes place
Style — Visual style, film stock, color palette
```

**Pro Tips:**

- **Write sentences, not keywords.** "A woman in a red dress" → "A confident woman in her 30s wearing a flowing crimson evening gown, looking directly at the camera with a slight smile"

- **Name the camera.** "Shot on Hasselblad X2D 135mm at f/5.6" produces radically different results than "portrait"

- **Direct the light.** "Soft key light from upper left" or "golden hour backlight through floor-to-ceiling windows"

- **Provide context.** Saying "for a luxury perfume launch campaign" changes the entire mood

- **Specify resolution.** End prompts with "4K output, 16:9 aspect ratio"

## Character Consistency Workflow

The workflow that actually works:

**Step 1:** Create strong character reference sheets. Clear, well-lit headshot or full-body.

**Step 2:** Upload reference images. Up to 14 total (4 characters + 10 objects).

**Step 3:** Use consistent descriptions. Same physical details across every prompt.

**Step 4:** Multi-image prompt structure. Upload all references alongside scene description.

**Step 5:** For video workflows, generate character sheets with multiple angles (front, left profile, right profile).

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Keyword stuffing** — "beautiful, stunning, amazing, incredible woman" doesn't help

❌ **Vague prompts** — "A cool scene" gives you generic results

❌ **Ignoring aspect ratio** — Cropping after generation loses quality

❌ **No lighting direction** — Default lighting is flat and boring

❌ **Skipping the "why"** — Context improves everything
