The question most people actually want answered:

**What are real businesses doing with this thing?**

The answer is more concrete and more varied than most expect. Here's a breakdown of use cases working in production right now, with costs and where the limits are.

## Marketing & Content Operations

### Automated Campaign Reporting

Marketing analysts spend hours logging into Google Ads, Meta, LinkedIn Campaign Manager to pull performance data. An OpenClaw agent navigates each platform, extracts metrics, and consolidates everything into a single report.

**The math:**
- Manual: 4-6 hours/week × $50/hr = $200-300/week
- OpenClaw: $10-20/month in tokens
- **ROI: Immediate**

### Competitor Monitoring on Autopilot

Weekly agent scrapes competitor websites for pricing changes, new features, press mentions. Formats a structured change log and sends it to Slack Sunday evening.

**Cost:** $10-15/month for 5-10 competitors
**Output:** Standing briefing that used to require an analyst

### Multi-Agent Content Pipeline

Work passes sequentially between specialist agents:
1. Research agent pulls sources and extracts data
2. Writing agent drafts from research
3. Review agent checks tone, SEO, facts
4. Scheduling agent queues output

**Result:** Content arrives review-ready, not rewrite-ready.

### Lead Enrichment at Scale

Give the agent a list of company names. It searches each record, extracts employee count, revenue, industry, tech stack, and writes results to your CRM.

**Cost:** $10-20/month for 100-200 leads/week
**Replaces:** Hours of manual LinkedIn and Clearbit lookups

## Developer Operations & DevOps

### Intelligent CI/CD Monitoring

Build fails. Agent detects it, fetches logs, analyzes the cause, sends a structured message:

> "Build #432 failed — 3 unit tests failing in auth module — likely caused by API change in commit abc123"

Instead of raw alerts + log diving, you get a diagnosis.

### Automated First-Pass Code Review

Agent monitors new PRs, runs review against your standards, flags common issues (missing error handling, inconsistent naming, security risks), leaves structured comments.

Human reviewer still approves. Agent handles the mechanical first-pass.

### Custom Monitoring Script Generation

Describe what you want to track in natural language. Agent writes and deploys the monitoring script.

**For teams with monitoring backlog:** Weeks of work compressed to hours.

## Sales Operations & CRM

### Automated Call Logging

Agent transcribes sales calls, extracts:
- Pain points mentioned
- Commitments made
- Next steps
- Objections raised

Logs structured notes to Salesforce or HubSpot automatically.

**Time saved:** 15-20 minutes per call
**For 20 calls/week:** 5-7 hours recovered

### Follow-Up Sequence Automation

After transcription, agent drafts personalized follow-up based on what was actually discussed — specific to the prospect's stated concerns. Rep reviews and approves instead of writing from scratch.

### Pipeline Hygiene on Autopilot

Weekly agent identifies stale opportunities, flags them with context summary, generates prioritized outreach list.

**Output:** "Pipeline health" report every Monday without pulling it manually.

## MSP & IT Operations

### Security Alert Triage

Agent connects to SIEM, processes overnight alerts, classifies by severity, cross-references against runbooks, generates morning report per client.

**Before:** Engineer manually reviews every alert at 7 AM
**After:** Pre-triaged report — critical items first, low items in weekly summary

### Automated Ticket Management

Agent monitors PSA (ConnectWise, Autotask), detects new tickets, cross-references knowledge base, drafts resolution.

For common recurring issues, resolves and closes without human intervention.

**Impact:** Even 20% automated resolution rate = significant operational leverage

### Client Briefing Automation

Weekly reports that took 30-45 minutes each generated automatically. Agent pulls PSA data, summarizes in plain English, formats to client preference.

## Finance & Trading

### Morning Market Briefings

Agent runs before market open:
- Overnight price movements
- Macro data releases
- News on watched positions
- Social sentiment signals

Synthesized into plain-English briefing delivered to Telegram.

**Replaces:** 45-60 minutes of manual research

### Invoice Processing

Receipts arrive by email or folder drop. Agent uses OCR, extracts data, logs to accounting system, sends confirmation.

**For 50-100 invoices/month:** Eliminates repetitive manual task entirely

### Financial Research Synthesis

Agent ingests annual reports, earnings calls, analyst reports, news. Extracts key metrics, identifies risks, compares YoY performance.

**Tasks that took a junior analyst a full day → minutes**

## Meetings & Knowledge Work

### Meeting Transcription + Action Extraction

Agent joins calls via OpenUtter, processes transcript after:
- Decisions made
- Action items with owners
- Open questions
- Dates mentioned

Posts structured output to Slack/Notion within minutes.

**Bonus:** Query transcript mid-meeting — "What has been decided so far?"

### Knowledge Base Q&A

Agent connects to internal docs, past transcripts, SOPs. Team asks natural language questions, gets answers with citations.

**Replaces:** 20-minute Confluence searches

## The Honest Limits

OpenClaw isn't right for everything:

- **Unstable interfaces** — Workflows depending on stable UIs are more reliable than scraping sites that change frequently
- **Heavy scale** — Above 50K AI calls/month, tokens can exceed $100 and dedicated platforms may be more economical
- **Regulated workflows** — Anything requiring audit trails needs careful design
- **Real-time requirements** — Agent latency matters for time-sensitive operations

---

## The Bottom Line

OpenClaw stops being a novelty when it starts saving money.

The use cases above share a pattern: they automate work that's:
1. Repetitive
2. Rule-based (even if the rules are complex)
3. Valuable enough that manual labor costs more than tokens

Find your version of that pattern. Start small. Measure the ROI.
