## The Context Problem is Dead

Every AI workflow has the same friction point: context transfer.

You're on a website. You want your AI to help. So you copy the URL, paste it into Claude, explain what you're looking at, maybe grab a screenshot. By the time you've recreated the context, you've lost the momentum.

Opera just killed that workflow.

With MCP Connector, your AI client — Claude, ChatGPT, OpenClaw, n8n, Lovable — connects directly to your live browser session. It sees what you see. Your open tabs. The content on screen. Your logged-in sessions.

This isn't a simulation. It's your real browser.

---

## What MCP Actually Is

MCP stands for Model Context Protocol. Anthropic introduced it in late 2024 as an open standard for AI tools to communicate with external services.

Before MCP, every integration was custom. Want Claude to talk to Notion? Build an integration. Want it to talk to Slack? Another integration. Every combination meant more code.

MCP standardizes this. Any AI tool that supports the protocol can connect to any MCP server instantly. No custom builds required.

Opera just exposed an MCP endpoint for its Neon browser. That means:

- **Claude** can browse inside your session
- **ChatGPT** can fill out forms on sites you're logged into
- **OpenClaw** can automate workflows across authenticated pages
- **n8n** can trigger browser-based actions in automation flows
- **Lovable** can prototype against live interfaces

One standard. Infinite use cases.

## What Your AI Can Actually Do

Once connected, external AI clients can:

- **Navigate pages** — Go to URLs, click links, scroll
- **Extract information** — Pull text, data, structured content from any page
- **Fill out forms** — Complete inputs on sites where you're already logged in
- **Capture screenshots** — Visual context for design or debugging
- **Open new tabs** — Expand research without manual clicking
- **Run searches** — Execute queries directly in the browser

The key difference: **authenticated context**. Your AI isn't scraping a public page — it's operating inside your logged-in session.

That means filling out forms on your banking site. Extracting data from your company's internal tools. Automating workflows on platforms that require auth.

## Why This Matters for OpenClaw Users

If you run OpenClaw, this is a direct upgrade to your browser automation stack.

Currently, browser automation with OpenClaw requires one of:
- Playwright/Puppeteer (simulated, headless)
- agent-browser (local CDP, but isolated sessions)
- Manual browser extension bridges

MCP Connector changes the architecture:

1. **Real session, real auth** — No more cookie injection or session recreation
2. **Live context** — AI sees exactly what you see, when you see it
3. **Bi-directional** — AI can both read and act within the browser
4. **Standard protocol** — Works with Claude, ChatGPT, and any MCP-compatible client

For workflows like:
- Scraping authenticated dashboards
- Filling multi-step forms across sites
- Researching while logged into paywalled content
- Testing web apps in real browser conditions

This removes entire categories of setup friction.

## The Agentic Browser Wars Are Heating Up

Opera isn't alone here. The browser-as-AI-execution-layer thesis is everywhere now:

- **Perplexity** launched Comet
- **OpenAI** launched Atlas
- **Norton** shipped Neo (yes, the antivirus company)
- **Opera** has Neon with Browser Operator since 2025

But Opera is the first to flip the model: instead of just embedding AI *into* the browser, they're letting AI tools reach *into* the browser from outside.

That's a fundamentally different bet. They're saying: the browser isn't the AI — the browser is the *execution layer* for AI.

## How to Get Started

MCP Connector is available today for Opera Neon subscribers.

1. Subscribe to Opera Neon (paid tier)
2. Enable MCP Connector in settings
3. Connect your AI client (Claude, ChatGPT, OpenClaw, etc.) via the MCP endpoint
4. Your AI now has live browser access

Opera mentioned they'll bring a simplified version to Opera One and Opera GX later.

## The Real Unlock

The shift here isn't technical — it's conceptual.

We've been treating AI as a thing you *go to*. Copy context, paste into chat, get response, copy back.

MCP Connector inverts this. The AI comes to *you*. It sees your workspace. It acts where you're already working.

"The browser is where workflows live, but AI has been disconnected from it," said Monika Kurczyńska, Opera's Director of R&D for browser AI. "With Opera Neon, we connect popular AI clients directly to an agentic browser, so they can operate where users already work."

This is what agentic AI looks like when it stops being a chatbot and starts being infrastructure.
