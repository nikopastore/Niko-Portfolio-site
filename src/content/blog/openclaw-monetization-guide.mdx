## 1. Sell Premium Skills on ClawHub

**Revenue: $100–$1,000/month per skill (passive)**

ClawHub is OpenClaw's official skill marketplace with 700+ skills and growing. Most are free, but premium skills sell for $10–200 each.

**What works:**
- Vertical-specific automations (Shopify inventory, real estate follow-ups, podcast production)
- Skills that solve expensive problems
- Clear documentation with real-world examples

**The math:**
- Build one skill that solves a $500/month problem
- Sell it for $49
- Get 20 sales = $980/month passive income
- No hosting costs — just a SKILL.md file

**Best sellers in 2026:**
- E-commerce inventory management
- Legal document drafting
- Real estate listing generation
- Customer support triage

---

## 2. Setup & Configuration Consulting

**Revenue: $500–$2,000 per client**

OpenClaw's creator says it's "not meant for non-technical users." That's your opportunity.

One solo consultant reported **$3,600 in month one** doing nothing but OpenClaw setups.

**What you offer:**
- Initial setup and security hardening
- Model routing optimization (most users burn money on Opus by default)
- Custom SOUL.md and memory configuration
- Integration with their existing tools

**Pricing:**
- Basic setup: $500
- Full configuration + 1 month support: $1,500
- Enterprise with custom skills: $2,000+

**Time investment:** 3-5 hours per setup once you have a system.

## 3. Managed OpenClaw Hosting

**Revenue: $50–$200/month per client (recurring)**

DigitalOcean, Alibaba Cloud, and Hostinger are all rushing to offer OpenClaw-as-a-Service. But they're competing on infrastructure, not expertise.

**Your edge:** White-glove service for non-technical users.

![Cloud server infrastructure](/blog/images/cloud-hosting.jpg)
*Managed hosting turns a one-time setup into recurring revenue.*

**What you offer:**
- Hosted OpenClaw instance (your VPS, their agent)
- Security updates and monitoring
- Cost optimization and alerts
- Skill installation and configuration
- Ongoing support

**Stack:**
- $5-10/month VPS cost
- Charge $100-200/month
- 10 clients = $1,000-2,000/month with 90%+ margins

## 4. OpenClaw Course or Tutorial Platform

**Revenue: $500–$5,000/month (passive after creation)**

The learning curve is brutal. That's content gold.

**Format options:**
- Gumroad course ($49-199)
- YouTube + Patreon
- Cohort-based bootcamp ($500-1,000)

**Modules that sell:**
- From zero to working agent (setup)
- Security hardening for non-paranoid people
- Cost optimization (how to run on $10/month instead of $100)
- Building your first custom skill
- Multi-agent orchestration

**Why it works:** OpenClaw's search volume is massive. Well-optimized content compounds.

Pre-built configurations that save hours of setup.

## 6. Automation-as-a-Service

**Revenue: $500–$1,500/month per client (recurring)**

Don't sell OpenClaw. Sell the outcome.

**Examples:**
- Daily competitor pricing reports
- Automated lead research and enrichment
- Content calendar generation and scheduling
- Customer support ticket triage

**How it works:**
- You run OpenClaw on your infrastructure
- Client gets deliverables (reports, data, content)
- They never touch the agent
- You charge for the service, not the tool

**Pricing model:**
- Fixed monthly fee based on deliverables
- Your API costs are COGS (~20-30% of revenue)
- Scale by templating workflows across clients

**Opportunities:**

## What NOT to Do

Skip the gray areas:

❌ **Multi-account automation** — Managing dozens of social/ad accounts via browser fingerprinting. Works until it doesn't. Platforms are getting smarter.

❌ **Scraping at scale** — Legal gray area. One cease-and-desist ruins your month.

❌ **"AI agency" with no specialization** — Too competitive. Niche down or die.

Focus on legitimate value creation. It's more sustainable and you sleep better.
